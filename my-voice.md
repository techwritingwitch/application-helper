# My Voice

This file helps the tailor-resume skill write in my style rather than generic AI prose.
Read this before writing any resume content.

---

## Writing style

- **Australian English** — organise, minimise, recognise, colour, behaviour. Never US spellings.
- **Direct and specific** — name the actual thing. "Forge Quest, an interactive gamified learning curriculum" not "an innovative educational initiative."
- **Numbers and titles where available** — "2,500+ participants", "Get the most out of Forge", "A Deeper Look at Hooks in Forge". Specifics build credibility; vague claims don't.
- **Em dashes for elaboration** — used to extend a point naturally, not to pad a sentence.
- **Drop the "I" in summaries** — "Passionate about building..." not "I am passionate about building..."
- **Parenthetical specifics** — comfortable with "(covering useState, useEffect, and async patterns)" — detail in parentheses rather than a separate sentence.
- **Personality is allowed** — the forgeTrain ("a Lego City Train triggered by Jira ticket events") is a good example: specific, a bit playful, but still professional.
- **Short sentences when landing a point** — don't pad.
- **Action verbs to open bullets** — Created, Authored, Wrote, Hosted, Built, Collaborated, Advised. Not "Responsible for" or "Involved in."

## Phrases and words that sound like me

- "make complex [X] accessible to [audience]"
- "at all experience levels"
- "hands-on"
- "fostering ongoing engagement"
- "reducing friction"

## Words and phrases to never use

These are AI tells — overused by language models and immediately recognisable as machine-written:

**Single words:** delve, leverage, utilise (use "use"), facilitate, underscore, tapestry, navigate, craft (as a verb), foster (unless quoting my own profile), curate, robust, seamless, holistic, elevate, empower, game-changer, cutting-edge, innovative, transformative, spearhead, synergy, impactful

**Phrases:** "plays a crucial role", "plays a vital role", "it is important to note", "it is worth noting", "in today's fast-paced world", "in the realm of", "when it comes to", "at the end of the day", "proven track record", "strong communication skills", "results-driven", "passionate team player", "go-to person", "think outside the box"

**Structural tells:** "Not just X — Y." / "No X. No Y. Just Z." / "Picture this..." / hollow rhetorical questions followed by a generic answer
